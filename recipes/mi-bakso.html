Mi Bakso

Photo by Muhammad Arifin Nursalim on Unsplash

Bakso, a popular comfort food in Indonesia, is found everywhere from street vendors to luxury hotels and upscale restaurants. This dish consists of brothy meatballs typically served with noodles, chili paste, and spring onions. But that's not all – Bakso is as adaptable as Nasi Goreng, with numerous preparation methods all centered around meatballs and broth. Its name comes from the Hokkien term bak-so (肉酥, Pe̍h-ōe-jī: bah-so·), meaning "fluffy meat" or "minced meat", reflecting its Chinese culinary origins. Like Nasi Goreng, the exact timeline of how this Chinese meatball soup became a staple in Indonesian cuisine remains unclear.

Different regions in Indonesia have their unique takes on Bakso. For instance, Bandung is known for ‘bakso cuanki’ (meatballs made with starch flour), while Malang is famous for ‘bakso bakar’ (grilled meatballs with broth). Despite these variations, Bakso remains a simple and versatile comfort food you can easily prepare at home during this stay-at-home period.

Ingredients

  • 5 pounds of beef bones
  • 1 knob of ginger, peeled
  • 4 cloves of garlic, smashed
  • 2-inch piece of cinnamon
  • 3 cardamom pods, pounded
  • 3 cloves
  • 6 black peppercorns
  • 18 meatballs
  • 3 cups of egg noodles, cooked
  • 1/2 cup crisp-fried shallots

How to Make

  1. Put the bones in a pot and add some water
  2. Bring to a boil and take off any scum that rises
  3. Add the ginger, garlic, cinnamon, cardamom, cloves, and peppercorns to the broth
  4. Simmer for at least two hours, then strain the broth and pour back into the pot without the bones and aromatics
  5. Heat the broth and bring to a simmer, then add the meatballs
  6. Divide the noodles among three bowls
  7. When everything is ready, pour the hot broth and meatballs directly into the bowls
  8. Top each bowl of meatballs with crisp-fried shallots
  9. Bakso is ready to be served.
