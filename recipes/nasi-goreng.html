Nasi Goreng

Photo by R Eris Prayatama on Unsplash

Nasi Goreng, literally meaning ‘fried rice’, is a highly versatile Indonesian dish with roots in Southern Chinese culture. It primarily consists of seasoned rice stir-fried with meat and vegetables, originally created as a way to use leftover rice. The exact timeline of when Indonesians adopted and modified Chinese fried rice into their own Nasi Goreng is unclear, but it is believed to have occurred around the 10th century during the flourishing trade between Indonesia and China under Sriwijaya’s reign. Today, nearly every region in Indonesia has its unique version of Nasi Goreng, such as Nasi Goreng Jawa, Nasi Goreng Aceh, and Nasi Goreng Padang. This beloved dish can be found on almost every street corner. Due to its versatility, if you have trouble finding certain ingredients, you can easily adapt the recipe with alternatives that suit your taste and are readily available.

Ingredients

  • 300 g of long-grain rice, cooked and then cooled overnight
  • 3 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 4 large eggs, beaten
  • 1 tablespoon of sweet soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • A pinch of salt
  • Pieces of finely-chopped proteins, like chicken breast, meat, shrimps, or anything you prefer

Spices

  • 200 g finely-cut shallots
  • 20 g finely-chopped garlic
  • 20 g of finely-chopped spring onions (or you can substitute it with leek or chives)
  • 3 red chilies, finely chopped

How to Make

  1. Stir-fry all the spices in vegetable oil until fragrant
  2. Then add the beaten eggs and a pinch of salt, and stir well
  3. Before the eggs get cooked, quickly add the proteins and the rice that was cooled overnight
  4. Stir again for a while
  5. When the flavors start to combine with each other, add the soy sauce and sweet soy sauce, and stir well
  6. Nasi Goreng is ready to be served.
